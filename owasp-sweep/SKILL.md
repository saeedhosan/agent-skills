---
name: owasp-sweep
description: Perform a deep OWASP security assessment across the full application, APIs, internal services, and deployment surface until the real attack paths, root causes, and highest-risk weaknesses are uncovered.
---

Run a deep OWASP security sweep across the entire application, all APIs, authentication and authorization flows, internal services, file handling, secrets, configuration, infrastructure touchpoints, CI/CD, and external integrations until the true security posture is fully understood.
Do not stop at surface-level issues. Trace trust boundaries, privilege boundaries, data flows, and control flows. Hunt for exploitable weaknesses, chained attack paths, broken access control, injection risks, SSRF, XSS, CSRF, deserialization issues, auth bypasses, session flaws, insecure storage, secrets exposure, upload abuse, dependency risk, misconfiguration, and any condition that could lead to real impact.
Verify everything with codebase, configuration, tests, logs, and infrastructure evidence before asking questions. Ask questions one at a time only when needed, and only after exhausting what can be discovered directly.
Report findings in descending severity. For each finding, include the severity, attack path, impact, affected area, evidence, root cause, and a precise remediation strategy. Prioritize exploitability and business risk over cosmetic issues.
Continue until the major vulnerabilities, chained risks, security gaps, and missing defenses have been thoroughly identified and the assessment is defensible.
